
Hydrogen is the most abundant element in the universe.
Helium is the second most abundant.
in a star, 4 hydrogen nucleii become helium with the reacion:

4Hp1n1 ->1Hep2n4 + 2b + 2g 

at 2e8 K, helium begins to form beryllium

2Hep2n4 ->1Bep4n8

1Hep2n4 + 1Bep4n8 ->1Cp6n12

1Hep2n4 + 1Cp6n12 ->1Op8n16

1Hep2n4 + 1Op8n16 -> 1Nep10n20 

1Hep2n4 + 1Nep10n20 ->1Mgp12n24

at 7e8 K

2Cp6n12 -> 1Na11n23 + 1Hp1n1 

1Cp6n12 + 1Op8n16-> 1Si14n28 + g

1Hep2n4 + 1Mgp12n24 -> 1Si14n28

1Hep2n4 +  1Si14n28 -> 1S16n32

1Hep2n4 +  1S16n32-> 1Ar18n36

1Hep2n4 +  1Ar18n36-> 1Cap20n40

increases temperature to 3e9

after that, towards the end of the life of a star, before a supernova
1Hep2n4 +  1Cap20n40->1Tip22n44 

1Hep2n4 +  1Tip22n44-> 1Crp24n48

1Hep2n4 +  1Crp24n48-> 1Fe26n52 

1Hep2n4 +  1Fe26n52->1Ni28n56  

1Hep2n4 +  1Ni28n56-> 1Zn30n60
